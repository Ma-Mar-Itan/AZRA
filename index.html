/ 04 — Swarm Intelligence Layer

From isolated drones to coordinated autonomous networks.

AZRA architects swarm-class autonomy at the protocol level — not as a feature, but as the operating principle that defines how every airframe behaves in formation.

Each drone is an autonomous node. The swarm is the system.

AZRA platforms are designed around distributed swarm architectures. Every airframe operates as an independent autonomous node, sharing sensing, positioning, and task allocation across a low-latency mesh — not directed from a central point of failure, but coordinated as a peer network.

If a node degrades or is lost, the network re-allocates and persists. Coverage holds. Mission integrity holds. Decision authority remains in the swarm.

The result is wide-area situational awareness, structural redundancy, persistence over time, and mission coverage that scales with the number of units in the air.

SW-01 / DA

Distributed Autonomy

Decision-making is local to each node. No central controller, no single point of failure.

SW-02 / MC

Mesh Communication

Encrypted peer-to-peer links with self-healing topology and adaptive routing under interference.

SW-03 / FT

Fault-Tolerant Coordination

Lost or degraded nodes are reassigned in seconds. The swarm reconverges around remaining capability.

SW-04 / AF

Adaptive Formation Control

Geometry shifts continuously to match terrain, mission phase, sensing demand, and threat posture.
